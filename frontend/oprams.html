Evaluation of risk — The procedure is outlined below.
RISKS — DEFINED AS THE LIKELIHOOD SOMETHING WILL HAPPEN AND HOW SEVERE THAT EVENT IS

Likelihood — of undesired events

LOW It has not happened for a long time, is known to be infrequent and is not likely to happen.
MEDIUM It may be less regular but is still recognised as being likely to happen.
HIGH It may happen regularly or could be a usual or common occurrence.

Severity — of undesired events

LOW Minor injuries to persons or some slight damage to property. Minimal environmental damage.
MEDIUM Serious injuries or persons off work for over three days, substantial damage. Damage contained within site boundaries but requiring prompt remedial action.
HIGH Fatal accident or multiple injuries / major property damage. Significant environmental impact, off-site damage or long-term issue.

Evaluation of the risk
Aim To reduce the risk to LOW where possible.
List the hazards associated with an activity Review the task / activity and estimate using LOW (L), MEDIUM (M) or HIGH (H) the likelihood of harm. Then do the same for severity using the same metrics.
Results
  • Combined risk = LOW: activity does not require further attention beyond standard good-practice controls and site rules.
  • Combined risk = MEDIUM: activity is significant, manage through method statements / additional procedures. Extra training or resources may be required.
  • Combined risk = HIGH: deemed significant, senior management control is high priority. Consider design changes, adopt best practice, manage through method statements and specialist planning.
